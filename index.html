About Caste Crimes

Caste-based crimes have been a pervasive and deeply rooted issue in India for centuries, reflecting societal discrimination and inequality. This data visualization project aims to shed light on the prevalence and trends of caste-related crimes in India from the years 2001 to 2014. By leveraging a comprehensive dataset obtained from official crime records and government reports, this project will employ data visualization techniques to provide a nuanced understanding of the dynamics surrounding caste-based crimes during this period.

In India, the caste system still causes discrimination and violence. Despite laws against it, millions suffer. The most affected are the Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe, while others enjoy privileges. Caste-based violence continues in various forms. Over 165 million people face cruelty and discrimination, just because of their caste. This unfairness happens from birth, no matter what religion you follow. Caste divisions permeate society, affecting housing, marriages, jobs, and daily interactions. They are reinforced through social exclusion, economic boycotts, and even physical violence. Through data visualization, we aim to uncover patterns, regional disparities, and temporal trends within these attributes. By translating complex data into visually compelling insights, we seek to raise awareness, facilitate informed discussions, and advocate for change.
